Metal organic Frameworks (MOF)

Metal organic frameworks are one of the highest surface area materials ever created. MOFs are organic-inorganic hybrid crystalline porous materials that consist of a regular array of positively charged metal ions surrounded by organic 'linker' molecules. Linker are connected together with metallic ions or clusters, called nodes

Get Started With Linkers

Linkers

Linkers are organic molecules that serve as bridges between the metal ions or clusters in Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs).

Common types of linkers include:

  • Dicarboxylic acids (e.g., oxalic acid, malonic acid, succinic acid, glutaric acid, terephthalic acid)
  • Tricarboxylic acids (e.g., citric acid, trimesic acid)
  • Azoles (e.g., 1,2,3-triazole, pyrrodiazole)
  • Amines, nitrates, sulfates, phosphates, and sulfonates

MOFs typically have extremely high porosity and surface area, with pores and cavities in the micropore range (less than 2 nm) or mesopore range (2-50 nm). The vast tunability of pore shape and size is made possible by the almost infinite selection of metals and linkers.

The variety of organic molecules that can act as linkers provides flexibility and stability in modifying MOF properties. By changing the chemical composition, length, and functional groups of the linker molecules, scientists can introduce specific functionalities into the MOF structure.

One of the many beneficial qualities of MOFs is their high surface area, which results from their cage-like structure. This structure can function as a sieve to specifically capture or adsorb particular chemical species.

MOF 177

A framework consisting of a [Zn4O6]6+ cluster and linker 1,3,5-benzenetribenzoate (BTB) ligands,

PROPERTIES OF MOF 177


Molecular Weight: 1148.37
Molecular Formula: C54H30O13Zn4
Appearance: Grey /Off-white Powder
Storage: Store at room temperature or 4 °C in dry-state. Recommend to be activated at 120 ℃ in a vacuum oven for 12 hours before use
Pore Volume: 1.6 cm³/g
Pore Size: 1.1 nm
Specific Surface: 3500-4000 m²/g (by BET)
Stability: Thermal stability: > 400°C; Stable in dry conditions for several weeks or several months
Metal: Zn
Ligand: 1,3,5-Tri (4-carboxyphenyl)benzene (CAS: 50446-44-1)

Synthesis of MOF 177


MOF -177 is one of the most porous materials whose structure is composed of octahedral Zn4O(−COO)6 and triangular 1,3,5-benzenetribenzoate (BTB) units to make a three-dimensional extended network based on the qom topology.

The synthesis of Zn-MOF-177 involves:

  1. Zinc Precursor: Zinc salts such as zinc nitrate hexahydrate (Zn(NO3)2•6H2O) are commonly used.
  2. Linker:1,3,5-benzenetribenzoate (BTB) serves as the organic linker.
  3. Solvent: solvent mixture of water and an organic solvent like N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) or N,N-dimethylacetamide (DMA) is typically used.
  4. Synthesis Steps:
    • The zinc precursor, 1,3,5-benzenetribenzoate, and the solvent mixture are combined in specific ratios under controlled conditions.
    • The mixture is then heated and stirred at a particular temperature for a defined duration to facilitate the formation of Zn-MOF-177 crystals.
  5. Crystallization and Isolation:
    • As the reaction proceeds, Zn-MOF-177 crystals start forming.
    • The mixture is cooled or allowed to stand to promote crystal growth.
    • The solid crystals are separated, washed with suitable solvents, and dried to obtain the final Zn-MOF-177 material.
  6. Characterization:
    • Characterization techniques such as X-ray diffraction (XRD), microscopy (SEM/TEM), and nitrogen adsorption-desorption isotherms are used to analyze the structure, morphology, surface area, and porosity of the synthesized Zn-MOF-177.

Application of MOF 177


  1. Gas storage and Separation – MOF-177 exhibits high surface area and tunable pore sizes, making it suitable for gas storage and separation. It has been studied for its potential in storing gases like hydrogen and methane, as well as separating mixtures of gases.
  2. Adsorption – MOF-177’s large surface area and pore structure allow it to adsorb various molecules, making it useful for applications like gas purification and removal of pollutants from water and air.
  3. Catalysis- MOF-177 acts as a catalyst in various chemical reactions. Its structure and porosity enable efficient catalytic reactions, making it useful in organic synthesis and chemical transformations.
  4. Drug delivery – MOF-177 can be modified to deliver drugs due to its porous structure.
